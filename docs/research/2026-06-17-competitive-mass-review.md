## Executive summary

The productized-local-website market is **overwhelmingly recurring-revenue**: subscription builders, managed-WordPress shops, AI generators, and vertical SaaS all keep the client paying and (usually) keep the site hostage. **Studio0rbit's exact model — one-time fee + full client ownership + no maintenance contract — is genuinely rare.** That is the real, defensible position.

**But the sharpest finding from the scans is a warning:** the things we'd planned to lead with — a **free-audit funnel**, **ownership messaging**, and a **self-edit CMS** — are **not differentiators.** The best one-time competitors already run audit funnels and shout "you own everything," and a self-edit CMS turns out to be the *norm*, not the exception. What's left that's actually uncommon is the **combination** (one-time + full ownership + no recurring fee) **plus design quality + performance (Astro) + local SEO schema.** Positioning and pricing should be rebuilt around that.

## What's genuinely ours (verified across scans)

1. **One-time + full ownership + no maintenance contract, combined.** Individually common; **together, rare.** Only Agency7 and Edmonton Web Design Co match the full combo, and both are Edmonton, higher-priced/multi-page, and not design-led.
2. **Design quality via a real design system.** No verified competitor leads on design rigor — most are template-grade. This is the least-contested high ground (and the CLAUDE.md "no AI slop" bar is built for exactly this).
3. **Astro performance + LocalBusiness JSON-LD as standard.** The cheap peers (Local Web Creator, Sitebolt) don't advertise schema; the subscription players carry platform bloat.

## Where we're exposed (devil's advocate)

1. **Our planned "differentiators" aren't.** Free-audit funnel (Agency7, Sitebolt, Fairway, Clio all run one; Insites sells it as a product), ownership messaging (Sitebolt/Agency7/Simple Built foreground it), and self-edit CMS (the *norm* — UENI/Jottful/My PT/Flavor Plate/Salon SEO all ship one). → Stop marketing these as edges.
2. **Price anchoring is real and below us.** Local Web Creator $249, Agency7 $497, Simple Built $599 — all one-time, all claiming ownership, all under our $1,800 Starter. We're premium with **zero clients/proof yet.**
3. **No recurring revenue cushion.** Everyone else has one. Our model lives or dies on **volume + turnaround**, which the pipeline must actually deliver.
4. **The market accepts "no self-edit."** Sitebolt and Simple Built sell done-for-you with no CMS and still win — so our Storyblok investment is a *nice-to-have*, not the wedge we hoped.

## White space (verified gaps)

- **No vertical-specific shop runs a true one-time/client-owns model.** Every vertical player (fitness, restaurant, salon, trades, law, dental, real estate) is subscription/hybrid. **We could own a vertical with our exact model** — and we already have barber (chopchop) + café demos to lead with.
- **"Design-led + you own it + one-time" is an unclaimed combination** in the local market — Agency7 owns "cheap + owns it," nobody owns "beautiful + owns it."

---

## Recommendations (ranked)

1. **Re-anchor positioning** on *"a beautifully designed site you own outright, once"* — design quality + true ownership + no recurring fee. Drop "we give you a CMS" and "free audit" as headline claims (keep them as table-stakes hygiene).
2. **Resolve the price-anchor question.** Either (a) add a true **1-page express tier** to deny the $249–599 crowd the cheap win, or (b) hold premium and make the **TCO + design gap** undeniable (a $1,800 one-time vs $75/mo breaks even in ~2 yrs, then it's free — and it's not template-grade). Recommend deciding this in roadmap Phase 2.
3. **Win a vertical with our model** (white space). Start with **barber/café** (we have demos); be the only "design-led, one-time, you-own-it" shop for that niche.
4. **Manufacture proof.** Every strong peer has case studies/testimonials; we have none. First 1–2 clients at a testimonial-for-discount deal; package before/after with metrics (à la NuTab's chopchop case study).
5. **Study Agency7 as the direct benchmark** — clone the good (5-tier ladder, audit+calculator+consult funnel, optional non-binding care plans), beat them on design.
6. **Weaponize the contrast.** Use Hibu / subscription lock-in ("you're renting; you don't own the code") as the explicit foil in sales material.
7. **Offer an optional, non-binding care/tune-up tier** (like Agency7's) — captures the hand-holding buyer without breaking the no-maintenance core.
