# Competitive Landscape & Market Proof

**Date:** 2026-06-16 · **Status:** living doc — update as new competitors/anchors surface.
**Purpose:** Who else does what we do, what works for them, where we win/lose, and the market proof that this trend is real. Source links live in [`references/links.md`](../references/links.md).

> TL;DR — The productized local-shop-website trend is real and already has Calgary players. **NuTab Digital is our closest direct competitor and, notably, built the *real* Chop-Chop Barber site** — the same shop behind our own before/after demo. Our wedge vs. them: AI pipeline + design catalogue (speed/volume), a free AI-audit outreach funnel, transparent pricing, and a true "own everything, no maintenance" story. Our gaps vs. them: published case studies with metrics, social proof, named-team trust, and a visible optional care plan.

## The chopchop overlap (our accidental benchmark)

NuTab built the live Chop-Chop Barber site ([chopchopbarber.com](https://chopchopbarber.com/)) — migrated off Squarespace to Next.js, PageSpeed 55→100, Lighthouse 100s, Setmore booking, 175+ Google reviews, click-to-call, story-driven. We independently built a chopchop before/after demo (`docs/screenshots/chopchop-*`). That gives us a direct head-to-head benchmark of our rebuild vs. a competitor's shipped, paid build.

⚠️ **Chop-Chop is NuTab's actual client.** Use it only as an *internal* quality benchmark — do **not** publish it as a poach-y public case study. For public proof, run the same treatment on 2–3 non-competitor Calgary shops.

- **Public case studies with hard metrics + before/after tables:** ChopChop Barber (55→100 PageSpeed, FCP 0.8s, Lighthouse 100s), KDT Restaurant (online ordering), King's Eye Towing (24/7 emergency service).

### GoHighLevel — [gohighlevel.com](https://www.gohighlevel.com) · PLATFORM, not a direct rival
- All-in-one agency SaaS: CRM, funnels, websites, booking, email/SMS automation, reputation, white-label. $97/mo Starter, $297/mo Unlimited. Built for *agencies*, not end shops. A tool we could resell — not a competitor in our lane.

### Araptus · productized, subscription model
- $900 setup + **$100/mo**, Astro.js, 90+ PageSpeed. Same productized-Astro idea as us but recurring. Validates the trend.

### Market price anchors (2026)
- Most professional small-biz builds land **$3k–$15k**; DIY builders $0–35/mo; full agencies $35k+. Our $1,800 / $3,500 / $6,000 one-time sits mid-market. **Note the low-end pressure: NuTab entry $900, Araptus $900+$100/mo — both undercut our $1,800 Starter.**

## Where we win (lean in)

- **Radical ownership / no lock-in** — "own everything, pay us nothing again" directly counters their retainer model. Sharpest wedge against an agency.
- **Transparent pricing + browsable gallery** — they hide both. A prospect sees a design close to their business before talking to anyone.
- **Free AI audit door-opener** — they have no equivalent; lead with a useful artifact, not a cold pitch. Scales street-by-street.
- **Pipeline speed/volume** — token-swap + content fill vs. their 2–12 wks of custom work. Margin + turnaround advantage they can't match without rebuilding their process.

## Where we're missing (close these)

1. **Published case studies with hard metrics** — their biggest weapon, our biggest hole. Package 2–3 non-competitor before/afters in their PageSpeed/Lighthouse table format.
2. **Social proof** — zero testimonials (zero clients yet). First 1–2 launches priced-to-close in exchange for testimonial + Google review.
3. **Named-team / local trust** — add founder face + Calgary-local signal to the landing page.
4. **Visible optional care plan** — pure no-maintenance is a great story but loses hand-holding buyers. Surface the existing à-la-carte tune-up/care option (see [`gtm/packaging.md`](../gtm/packaging.md)) rather than burying it.

## Where we shine in local communities

- **Neighbourhood-by-neighbourhood, vertical-by-vertical** — the 60-design catalogue means barber/café/spa designs are already made for Renfrew, Inglewood, Kensington. Pair with [`gtm/vertical-content-kits.md`](../gtm/vertical-content-kits.md).
- **Audit-first outreach at scale** — audit a whole street, send branded 1-pagers; a quote-by-contact agency can't.
- **"You own it forever"** — resonates with owners burned by monthly fees and agency hostage situations.

# Deep research — wider competitor set & devil's advocate (2026-06-16)

*Method: multi-agent deep-research harness — 5 search angles → 23 sources → 106 claims → 25 adversarially verified (3-vote, need 2/3 to kill) → 15 confirmed, 6 after dedup. Rate-limiting forced ~10 claims to abstain (unverified, **not refuted** — see "Unverified" below). Pricing is vendor marketing copy, time-sensitive (2026), and headline rates usually require annual pre-pay.*

## The dominant pattern is the inverse of us

The most visible players all run **recurring subscriptions that bundle hosting + domain into an agency-owned account** — soft lock-in, the structural opposite of our one-time / client-owns model. That makes our ownership story genuinely differentiated, but it also means we're swimming against the prevailing buyer expectation ("$X/mo and you handle everything").

| Competitor | Model | Pricing (verified) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **B12** | Subscription (DIY + DIFY) | $49/mo Website · $78/mo +engagement · DIFY **$199–$399/mo + $1,999 setup** | DIFY bundles hosting, AI credits, scheduling, edit requests; Advanced adds ongoing SEO + monthly blog content |
| **Squidstart** | Pure subscription | **$249–$749/mo**, "no setup, no contracts" | Unlimited updates + hosting + SSL in every tier |
| **Websites.ca** (Calgary) | Managed WordPress sub | **$75/mo** (annual pre-pay), no setup fee | Bundles design + hosting + domain + 5 emails + SEO + support |
| **Wix** | DIY subscription | **$24 / $36 / $46 / $172 /mo** (+ free tier) | Self-serve builder |
| **Zarla** | Freemium AI builder | free templates → ~$3 intro to $12–24/mo | Free vertical template galleries as the funnel |
| **Simple Built Co.** | **One-time** (closest to us) | **$599 first page + $150/page** | Client owns outright, no contracts; optional paid hosting exists |

## What competitors do that our plan does not

1. **They monetize the relationship we give away.** B12/Squidstart bundle *unlimited edits, managed hosting, SEO, monthly blog content* into the subscription — recurring revenue + stickiness. We forgo all of it by design. Trade-off is real: no residual income cushion, so **volume + turnaround is our entire engine** (and we have zero clients yet — fragile).
2. **"Just text us, we fix it in a day or two."** Websites.ca calls WordPress "self-editable" but admits *almost all clients just email and the agency handles it fast*. **This is our single biggest weakness:** many local owners don't *want* to self-edit, and our no-maintenance model has **no answer** to the small-edit-after-launch request. "You own your CMS" can be a *weaker* sell than "we'll just do it."
3. **They productize the audit funnel.** Insites sells a white-label embeddable SEO-audit widget (one cited agency: 500 leads/mo). Our free-AI-audit funnel is **validated but not unique** — it's a commoditized tactic. We must out-execute on audit *quality + local specificity*, not assume the funnel itself is an edge.
4. **Free template galleries as top-of-funnel.** Zarla gives vertical templates away free to pull leads in. Our 60-template catalogue is a build-speed asset but **not a marketing moat** — don't over-index on "60 templates" in positioning. The moat is done-for-you build + ownership + design quality.

## Devil's advocate — where we're exposed

- **Price anchoring.** A $0-down / $75-mo offer and a $599 one-time build both *feel* cheaper to a cash-strapped local than $1,800 upfront — even when our multi-year TCO wins. Simple Built undercuts our entry, and a 6–7 page build there (~$1,400–1,500) converges on our $1,800 tier, eroding the premium gap. **We need either a low-anchor express tier or a hard TCO pitch** ($1,800 once vs $75/mo = breakeven ~2 yrs, then pure savings).
- **No "fix-it" safety net.** See #2 above — the most actionable gap. An *optional, à-la-carte* paid edit/care option (already in `gtm/packaging.md`, but buried) would capture the hand-holding segment **without** breaking the no-maintenance core.
- **Lock-in claim is inferred, not proven.** We position against subscription lock-in, but we haven't actually verified how hard it is to export/migrate a B12/Squidstart/Websites.ca site. Get proof before leaning on that line publicly.

## What's genuinely defensible (keep)

- **True ownership / no lock-in** vs the dominant subscription pattern — real and rare.
- **One-time TCO advantage** compounds hard over multiple years.
- **Calgary-local + design-system quality.**

## Unverified (rate-limited, abstained — re-research, do NOT treat as fact)

- **Mind2Market Group** — flagged as a *possible direct one-time-fee / client-owns analog*. Went 0-0 (abstain), not refuted. **Worth a dedicated follow-up.**

- Simple Built's "no required ongoing fees" framing was actively **refuted (0-2)** — an optional hosting subscription appears to exist; treat them as one-time-build + optional-hosting.
