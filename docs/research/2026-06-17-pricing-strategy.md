**Date:** 2026-06-17 · **Method:** deep-research harness, 5 angles → 22 sources → 96 claims → 25 verified (15 confirmed, 10 killed). Triggered by the question: should we have fewer tiers / lower prices given our fast, low-cost AI pipeline?

> **Bottom line:** The evidence pushes back on the "we're fast & cheap to build, so lower the prices and simplify" instinct. **Keep 3 tiers. Don't lower list prices.** Our build efficiency is margin to *keep*, not a discount to pass on. The right way to get lower effective prices while we have zero proof is a **time-bound founding-client discount**, not permanently cheaper list prices.

## What the user asked (and the honest answers)

**"Why a whole Express tier/page?"** → You're right — **don't add one.** A 4th tier works against us (choice overload on a high-stakes, hard-to-compare purchase). Starter *is* the entry tier. **Drop the Express idea.** ✅ instinct correct.

**"Can we just lower prices? They seem high, and we're fast."** → **This is the classic trap (verified).** Two independent mechanisms:
- *Value vs. effort pricing* (`high`, 3-0): when you price to effort/cost, AI efficiency becomes the *client's* gain; when you price to value, the efficiency surplus is *yours to keep*. Cutting a 20h job to 5h shouldn't cut the price 75% for the same/better output. Sources: digitalapplied, Thomson Reuters, Bessemer.
- *Low price lifts raw conversion but kills revenue-per-visitor* (`high`, 3-0), **and** *price is a quality signal* (`high`, 3-0; Rao & Monroe 1989, Völckner & Hofmann 2007) — too-low signals low quality and attracts worse clients. → **Being fast/cheap-to-build is NOT a reason to lower price.**

**"Why 3 tiers?"** → Good-better-best with 3 (best-practice 3–4) is the conventional, defensible count (`high`, 3-0; HBR 2018, Figma, Wikipedia GBB). Fewer than 3 loses the anchor/compromise lever. *Caveat: this research skews subscription/ecommerce; a solo one-time shop could run 2 tiers + an anchor — but 3 is sound.*

## Verified findings

1. **Keep 3 tiers** (`high`). 3–4 is best practice; avoids analysis paralysis. The lever that matters more than count: tiers must be **few AND clearly differentiated** — web design for an SMB owner is exactly the high-stakes / low-preference-clarity / hard-to-compare purchase where choice-overload bites (Chernev meta-analysis, 99 observations). Make each tier a distinct outcome, not three near-identical option lists.
2. **Top tier = genuine ANCHOR, not a manipulative decoy** (`high`). A high-priced top option pulls buyers to the safe middle via extremeness aversion / Goldilocks (Williams-Sonoma $429 bread maker ~doubling the $279 model). For quality-led, mission-critical work, **Blair Enns explicitly prefers the anchor over the decoy** ("decoys are slightly manipulative… for low-price high-volume"). → **Pro should be a real, richer high anchor**; **Growth the highlighted best-value middle (the target).**
3. **Price to value, not effort** (`high`). Keep the AI-pipeline margin. Don't discount because the build is cheap/fast.
4. **Risk asymmetry favors holding price** (`medium`). Too-LOW is the better-evidenced mistake (lost margin, quality-signal damage that's hard to reverse, worse clients, AI gains given away). Too-HIGH is a real conversion risk **but mitigable** because our **visible portfolio (60-template gallery) + free audit substitute for missing testimonials** — the price-quality signal is only moderate and weakens when buyers have other quality cues, which we have.
5. **Pricing-page mechanics** (`high`/synthesis): show all tiers side-by-side; "from $X" on the entry tier, single anchored prices on named tiers; highlight the middle as "Most Popular."

## Devil's advocate — steelmanning the "lower it" view (and why it still loses)

- Cold, with zero clients, a $3,500 ask is hard to close, and cheap one-time peers (Agency7 $497, Local Web Creator $249) plant a low anchor in the prospect's head. **True.**
- **But** racing the $249 shops is unwinnable and off-brand for a design-led studio, and permanent low prices are very hard to raise later. The fix isn't lower list prices — it's a **time-bound founding-client discount** (e.g. first 3–5 clients at ~30–40% off in exchange for a testimonial + case study). This gets lower *effective* prices now, builds the proof we lack, and preserves the anchor and quality signal.

## ⚠️ Honest caveat on the dollar figures

**No Calgary/Canada 2026 one-time price band survived verification in this run** — every competitor-pricing claim used to triangulate it was refuted here. (We *do* have those competitor prices verified in [`market-scan/scan-001`](market-scan/scan-001.md)–[`002`](market-scan/scan-002.md): Agency7 $497–4,997, Hexagon $2,500–9,000, Sitebolt $1,500/2,500, etc.) So **$1,800 / $3,500 / $6,000 is reasoned positioning** — above cheap AI/builders and discount one-time peers, below custom-agency ceilings — **not an evidence-locked band.** Sanity-check against live quotes before committing.

Refuted stats — do NOT cite: jam-study "10x", "3-tier converts 1.4x better", "48% of SaaS use 3 tiers", "62% of high-earning agencies value-price", Figma decoy-middle claim.

## Recommended structure

**Keep 3 one-time CAD tiers; no Express; don't lower list prices.**

| | Starter | **Growth** ⭐ (target) | Pro (anchor) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Price | **from $1,800** | **$3,500** | **from $6,000** |
| Role | credible entry | highlighted best value | genuine high anchor |
| Make it… | clearly the "good" | "Most Popular," the rational middle | richer scope (e-com/booking, copywriting, advanced SEO, multi-location) so Growth reads as the smart choice — a real upgrade, not a fake decoy |

- **Care:** per-change, pay-as-you-go (no recurring) — preserves the "$0/mo to us" headline.
- **Early-stage lever:** time-bound **founding-client discount** (first 3–5 clients) for testimonial + case study, instead of cutting list prices.
- **Lean on visible work** (gallery + audit) to carry quality signaling while social proof is thin.
