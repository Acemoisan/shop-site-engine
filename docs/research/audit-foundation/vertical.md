# Vertical Anatomy & Per-Vertical Must-Haves

Per-vertical conversion anatomy, trust signals, schema typing, booking patterns, and media slots across the nine Calgary local-shop verticals — synthesized from the `dimension: "vertical"` claims. Every claim in this set carries **provisional** confidence, so every point below is provisional; relative emphasis follows each claim's necessity and impact.

## Per-vertical must-haves

### Barber
- **Primary conversion is online booking**: `bookingLink` is a primary appointment-driven action for barber, and the single highest-value upgrade is an **embedded booking widget** (Fresha/Booksy/Square Appointments) rather than a bare link — detectable by iframe/script src to known hosts _(provisional)_.
- Ship a **portfolio/results gallery** (recent cuts, fades, beard work), style/skill demonstration rather than clinical before/after, pairing naturally with an Instagram feed for freshness under the no-maintenance model _(provisional)_.
- A **before/after** paired-media slot also applies (portfolio-style here), and a **Meet the Team** headshot+bio component is strong for barber _(provisional)_.
- Schema type: **HairSalon** — there is NO `BarberShop` type; barbers use `HairSalon` (parent `HealthAndBeautyBusiness`) _(provisional)_.

### Cafe / Restaurant
- **Menu/food photography is conversion-critical**: items with photos sell measurably more and ~84% of diners want food photos before choosing — the menu/Services component needs **per-item image slots**, not text-only _(provisional)_.
- `bookingLink` (ordering/reservation) is a primary action; online reservations lift restaurant website conversion ~19% _(provisional)_.
- Cafe is the one vertical where **content-specific Menu schema** is correct: `CafeOrCoffeeShop` subtype + `servesCuisine` + `hasMenu → Menu → hasMenuSection → hasMenuItem`. The Menu graph is machine-readability (not a guaranteed snippet) _(provisional)_.
- `menuSchema` is **only** meaningful for cafe/restaurant — it is dead weight for all other verticals and should be vertical-masked _(provisional)_.
- Interior/ambiance photography is conversion-relevant (atmosphere is the product) — expose an interior/space gallery slot _(provisional)_.

### Spa
- For **massage-offering spas**, a practitioner/RMT credential is a **must-have, conversion-relevant signal**: paid massage in Calgary requires a massage practitioner's licence (MTAA/NHPC/CRMTA membership) or a body-rub practitioner licence (min. 250 hrs); the centre is licensed separately. RMT designation also drives **insurance direct-billing** (a real conversion lever) — distinguish "massage therapy" (RMT, insurable) from "body rub" clearly _(provisional)_.
- A **before/after gallery** is conversion-critical (med-spa/cosmetic): it is the second-most-visited page after home, a primary CRO surface — ship a dedicated paired before/after media slot _(provisional)_.
- `bookingLink`/embedded **booking widget** is a primary action (30–35% of salon/spa calls go unanswered) — highest-value missing item for spa _(provisional)_.
- A **Meet the Team** headshot+bio component is strong for spa _(provisional)_.
- Correction: estheticians, nail techs and lash artists are NOT provincially licensed in Alberta (only hairstyling is a designated trade), so "licensed esthetician" is a marketing claim — lead instead with **AHS sanitation/infection-control approval** + accredited-program training as the trust signal _(provisional)_.
- Schema type: **DaySpa** or **BeautySalon** (`HealthAndBeautyBusiness`); interior/ambiance gallery slot is conversion-relevant (72% want ambiance imagery before booking) _(provisional)_.
- Canadian-built **Jane App** (from ~CAD $54/mo, brandable embeddable booking page) is the strong CAD-fit booking option for spas _(provisional)_.

### Trades
- **Proof of WCB Alberta coverage** (clearance letter / WCB number) is a must-have trust signal — Alberta.ca's "Hiring a contractor" guidance tells homeowners to ask for it; any construction business with workers must register _(provisional)_.
- A **"licensed & insured" statement** (public liability + property damage) is a near-universal expected signal; Alberta has no single general-contractor licence, so trade-specific certs + municipal business licence + insurance + WCB are the real signals ("Licensed, insured & WCB-covered" is the canonical line) _(provisional)_.
- A **quote/estimate request mechanism** (estimate form or instant-quote tool) is the high-value missing item — for trades it ranks alongside `clickToCall` as the primary CTA; contractor sites convert ~10–15% of visitors who provide contact info _(provisional)_.
- A **categorized project/job gallery** (work by service type, before/after, with location context) is conversion-critical visual proof of capability — and **license numbers / certifications / insurance / OEM badges** are a distinct credential-badge media slot, often more decisive than gallery polish _(provisional)_.
- Optional **location/caption field** on gallery items (city/neighborhood tagging, "serving [area]") raises trust and doubles as a Calgary "near me" local-SEO signal _(provisional)_.
- Schema type: a **HomeAndConstructionBusiness** subtype (`Electrician`/`Plumber`/`HVACBusiness`/`GeneralContractor`/`RoofingContractor`/`HousePainter`/`Locksmith`); `areaServed` is the high-value property for verticals that travel to the customer _(provisional)_.

### Fitness
- **Embedded class schedule/timetable is the core conversion engine**: a live, bookable, in-page class calendar (Mindbody from ~USD $99/mo, Glofox, Wodify from ~$79/mo) installed via a copyable code snippet that renders calendar + booking + account signup in-page; this is effectively mandatory for the vertical _(provisional)_.
- The **single PRIMARY headline CTA is a free-trial / intro-offer signup** (top-of-funnel); membership/package signup and per-class booking are secondary. Fitness runs a trial-led funnel (trial → recurring membership) layered on per-class booking _(provisional)_.
- Required anatomy: hero with free-trial CTA, bookable timetable, membership/pricing packages, trainer/instructor profiles, results/testimonials + reviews, facility gallery, hours, location/map, FAQ, recurring-billing membership signup, and schema _(provisional)_.
- **Certified-trainer credentials** (NCCA-accredited, e.g. NASM-CPT) are the primary expertise signal but a should-have, not a legal requirement — trainer certification is not provincially licensed in Alberta _(provisional)_.
- A **liability waiver / informed-consent** step belongs in the booking/intake flow (operational legal protection, not a public trust badge — niche as a website element) _(provisional)_.
- Schema type: **ExerciseGym** or **HealthClub** (`HealthClub` has dual parents `SportsActivityLocation` + `HealthAndBeautyBusiness`). Note: a recurring-class-schedule **Event rich result is not practically achievable** — Google's Event result is single-event-per-URL and ignores `eventSchedule` recurrence; treat Event as out-of-scope for the standard template _(provisional)_.
- A **Meet the Team** headshot+bio component is strong for fitness; facility/interior photos raise interest ~19% and address a top pre-commit anxiety _(provisional)_.

### Dental
- **Primary conversion is an online new-patient appointment request** (a request, not necessarily instant-confirm), supported by `clickToCall`, because new-patient onboarding precedes scheduling — distinct from a barber/cafe/spa transactional booking _(provisional)_.
- **Display Alberta regulatory registration** (Alberta Dental Association and College / College of Dental Surgeons of Alberta) as a must-have trust line; marketing must be truthful and not misleading, and "specialist"/"best dentist" claims are barred unless certified — put a registration line in footer/about _(provisional)_.
- **Listing accepted insurance / "direct billing"** is a high-impact, near-table-stakes conversion signal (cost/coverage is the dominant patient concern) and is frequently underused — ship a "direct billing / insurance accepted" band _(provisional)_.
- A **new-patient + insurance intake** layer (mobile-responsive digital forms: demographics, health history, consents, insurance card front/back upload) is the dental-specific second conversion layer; aim for completion ~24h before the visit _(provisional, should-have)_.
- Required anatomy: hero with appointment-request CTA + click-to-call, services (general/cosmetic/emergency), new-patient/insurance/financing info, appointment-request form, intake form, **team/doctor bios** (trust weighs heavily in healthcare; 92% read a clinician bio before booking, 77% rely on visual cues — needs headshot slots), reviews, hours, location/map, FAQ, and **Dentist** schema _(provisional)_.
- A **before/after gallery** (cosmetic/ortho) is conversion-critical but **consent-gated** — written patient consent is required before publishing; treat it as a client-populated slot, not scraped images _(provisional)_.
- Booking integration: prefer a PMS/EHR-synced platform (NexHealth most cited) added via copyable embed or hosted "Book Now" link — but NexHealth is a premium sales-quoted subscription (~USD $299–$350+/mo) better suited to established practices. Canadian-built **Jane App** (from ~CAD $54/mo) is a better CAD fit; realistic one-time-fee default is to embed the client's existing PMS link or a simple appointment-request form _(provisional)_.
- Schema type: **Dentist** (dual parent `LocalBusiness`/`MedicalBusiness` + `MedicalOrganization`); `medicalSpecialty` is a dental-only, machine-readability-only field (no dedicated medical rich result) _(provisional, nice-to-have)_.

### Law
- **Single primary conversion is a consultation / case-evaluation request** — a specific CTA like "Schedule a Free 15-Minute Case Evaluation" rather than generic "Contact Us", backed by click-to-call. No e-commerce, no transaction online _(provisional)_.
- **Organize around per-case-type practice-area pages** (personal injury, immigration, estate planning, family), each a dedicated conversion page with targeted, empathetic messaging mirroring the client's own problem language and a practice-area-specific intake form; a single broad "we handle everything" page underperforms — these "matter more than almost any other element" _(provisional)_.
- Required anatomy: home with single primary consultation CTA, practice-area pages, **attorney/team bios with credentials + bar memberships**, results/testimonials, short consultation/intake form, contact + click-to-call, and `LegalService`/`Attorney` schema. No menu, ordering, or e-commerce _(provisional)_.
- **Law Society of Alberta membership** is the foundational trust credential; the Code of Conduct (Chapter 4) requires all marketing be demonstrably true, accurate and verifiable _(provisional)_.
- **Avoid prohibited language** — "specialist"/"expert" (no Alberta specialist certification), superlatives ("best"/"top"/"leading"), and advertised settlement/verdict amounts — use "focuses on" / "extensive experience in" instead. This is a copy-level constraint baked into template content _(provisional)_.
- A **"past results do not guarantee future outcomes" disclaimer** is a conditional must-have — required only if the site references outcomes; ship a disclaimer slot near results/testimonials _(provisional)_.
- The dominant lead-capture pattern is **capture-first**: short contact/intake form (name, contact, brief description) + prominent click-to-call + a repeated "Free Consultation" CTA in sticky header and mid-page; many firms deliberately use no scheduler (intake/conflict-checking is by phone). Maps directly to existing `contactForm` + `clickToCall` — no embed needed for baseline _(provisional)_.
- Keep initial intake **short** (essential fields only) for higher completion; deeper intake happens after booking _(provisional, should-have)_. Optional self-serve booking via **Calendly** (free-tier embed: inline/popup/link) if a single attorney wants it _(provisional, nice-to-have)_.
- Media emphasis is **professional team/attorney headshots** (and secondary office imagery), **not** work galleries — prioritize the headshot slot, de-prioritize gallery slots _(provisional)_.

### Auto
- **Dual primary conversion**: a dominant above-the-fold "Schedule Service" appointment CTA **plus** a quote/estimate request, both backed by sticky click-to-call (booking is dominant; estimate-request is the auto-specific second path). Online booking aims for <5 clicks _(provisional)_.
- The homepage must instantly answer three questions — **"Can you fix my problem?"** (service list + makes serviced), **"Can I trust you?"** (real shop photos, reviews, certifications, warranties), **"How do I book?"** (dominant schedule/call CTA) _(provisional)_.
- An **AMVIC business licence** is mandatory to legally operate auto repair in Alberta; display the **AMVIC licence number** as a must-have trust signal _(provisional)_.
- A **quote/estimate request mechanism** is the high-value missing item — ranks alongside `clickToCall` as a primary CTA (customers want a price before committing, 24/7) _(provisional)_.
- A **categorized project/job gallery** + **before/after** (auto body) is conversion-critical visual proof, and **license/certification/OEM badges** are a distinct, often-decisive credential-badge slot _(provisional)_.
- Required anatomy: hero with Schedule/Call CTA + credibility subheading, services-with-pricing, makes/vehicles serviced, quote/estimate form, booking form, certifications/warranties, real shop photos, reviews, NAP + map/directions, service-area strip, FAQ, coupon/offer near CTA, and `AutoRepair` schema _(provisional)_.
- Should-haves: **transparent service menu with pricing** (price transparency is a stated expectation; maps to the Services component, estimate-request supplements where price varies); **technician trade certification** (Automotive Service Technician + optional Red Seal — display "Red Seal certified," not US-centric ASE); **warranty + financing** as risk-reducing conversion signals; optional location/caption on gallery items _(provisional)_.
- Booking: capacity-aware shop scheduler (AutoOps ~USD $100/mo, Shopmonkey) embeds a "Book Online" widget and pushes to GBP; one-time-fee default ships a quote-request form + click-to-call, with an embed slot if the client already pays for shop-management software _(provisional)_.
- Schema type: **AutoRepair** (parent `AutomotiveBusiness`); `areaServed` is high-value for mobile mechanics _(provisional)_.

### Retail
- **Menu/product photography**: the product showcase should default to real per-product image slots (real photos out-convert stock), but this is should-have rather than must-have because catalog-as-foot-traffic sites can convert on a curated subset _(provisional)_.
- Schema type: **Store**; `menuSchema` and `bookingLink` are masked out for retail — for retail the inventory should emphasize **reviews + addressOrMap + clickToCall** _(provisional)_.

## Cross-vertical patterns

- **The "lead/relationship" CTA divergence**: across the four newer verticals (law, dental, auto, fitness) the primary CTA is a LEAD/REQUEST or RECURRING-RELATIONSHIP action, **not** a one-off transactional booking/order — law = consultation request, dental = appointment request + intake, auto = appointment + estimate request, fitness = free-trial→membership. The "bookingLink" item is too narrow and should generalize to a "primary conversion action" covering consultation/appointment/estimate/trial, plus an intake-form item for dental/law _(provisional)_.
- **Two-layer trust model**: a **governing-credential layer** (table-stakes legitimacy: auto=AMVIC, law=Law Society, dental=ADA&C/CDSA, trades=WCB+insurance, massage=Calgary practitioner licence) and a **conversion-moving layer** (reviews, insurance-accepted/direct-billing, warranty, consented before/after). Esthetics/fitness have NO governing licence, so their signals are voluntary certifications. Regulated verticals (law strongest, dental next) also carry advertising-content constraints _(provisional)_.
- **Reviews/social proof are the universal top trust signal** — highest-impact across all verticals, outweighing credential badges (86% cite homepage 5-star ratings as the top first-purchase signal; 93% read reviews before purchasing; 92% need ≥4 stars). Weight highest for trust-sensitive dental/law/spa/auto. Note: 4.2–4.5 stars converts better than a perfect 5.0 _(provisional)_.
- **Real (non-stock) photography of the actual business/team/work** out-converts stock and is the baseline expectation everywhere — default all media slots to client-supplied photos _(provisional)_.
- **Testimonials/review widgets with attribution** (names, photos, live Google reviews) are a universal proof component default _(provisional)_.
- **Mobile-first + fast static delivery is universal** (`mobileViewport` full-weight everywhere; ~59–60% of sessions are mobile); the repo's static Astro + Core Web Vitals targets (LCP<2.5s/INP<200ms/CLS<0.1) fit all verticals _(provisional)_.
- **HTTPS is a hard trust gate** — failure is catastrophic (reported ~64–85% abandon on "Not Secure"); treat as near-pass/fail, not 1/12 of an average _(provisional)_.
- **Click-to-call is universal but PRIMARY for high-intent/urgent verticals** (trades, auto, dental, law) — ~70% of local-business calls now come from click-to-call, phone leads convert ~10–12× better than web-form leads; weight above generic items for these verticals, lower for retail/cafe _(provisional)_.
- **Hours + addressOrMap (NAP/map/directions)** are universal must-haves for any physical location (all nine verticals), commonly incomplete on prospect sites _(provisional)_.
- **Schema is one per-vertical knob, not per-shop optimization**: emit the **most specific `LocalBusiness` subtype** (Google's explicit recommendation), with a small map of conditional properties — `priceRange` (all), `servesCuisine`+`hasMenu` (cafe), `areaServed` (trades/auto/law), `makesOffer`/`OfferCatalog` for service verticals (model services as Service/OfferCatalog, NOT Product), `medicalSpecialty` (dental). The only guaranteed Google rich result is the knowledge-panel business details, requiring just **name + address**; everything else is machine/AI-readability (AEO/GEO), and structured data does **not** directly affect rankings _(provisional)_.
- **Schema guardrails**: the **FAQPage rich result is effectively dead** (deprecated; gone from Search ~May 2026) — keep FAQ for UX/AI-readability, don't market it as a SERP feature. **Self-authored `aggregateRating` on a shop's own site earns no review-snippet** — Google ignores self-serving reviews; the panel star rating comes from Google Business Profile, so don't inject fabricated review schema _(provisional)_.
- **Booking embeds collapse to three static-site primitives**: (a) a JS/code-snippet widget rendered in-page (Mindbody/Wodify/NexHealth button, Calendly inline/popup), (b) a hosted booking link/button (NexHealth Direct Link, AutoOps, **Reserve-with-Google** — free, just a URL), or (c) a plain HTML form (law intake, auto quote) — none require a backend. Architectural takeaway: add a per-vertical `BookingEmbed` content slot accepting `{embedCode}` or `{bookingUrl}` plus the existing form component _(provisional)_.
- **Media slots are vertical-specific, not one-size-fits-all**: paired before/after (spa, dental, auto body, barber-as-portfolio), categorized project gallery (trades, auto), per-item food photos (cafe), per-product photos (retail), facility/ambiance gallery (cafe, spa, fitness, dental), team headshots+bios (dental, law, fitness, barber, spa), credential/badge slots (trades, auto) — and explicitly **no gallery, yes headshots** for law _(provisional)_.

## Audit takeaways

- **Generalize the primary-conversion item per vertical**: score the right action — consultation request (law), appointment request + intake (dental), appointment + quote/estimate (auto/trades), free-trial→membership (fitness), embedded booking widget (barber/spa/dental/fitness), order/reservation (cafe). The current keyword `bookingLink` is too narrow.
- **Vertical-mask the inventory and re-weight**: exclude non-applicable items so they don't depress the denominator (the live bug — `menuSchema` is always-false for 8 verticals and caps an otherwise-perfect site; it applies only to cafe/restaurant). Apply per-vertical weights (primary-CTA items ~2×, trust/social-proof full, polish like `ogTags`/`favicon` ~0.25×) and add two universal hard gates: **https** and **mobileViewport**.
- **Add missing high-value, vertical-specific detectors**: embedded booking **widget** vs bare link (barber/spa/dental/fitness, detect known hosts: fresha, booksy, square, calendly, setmore, simplybook, mindbody, acuityscheduling, wodify, gymmaster, classfit, vibefam); **quote/estimate form** (trades/auto); **insurance-accepted/direct-billing block** (dental); **class/schedule timetable** (fitness); **practice-area pages + per-area CTA** (law).
- **Score `clickToCall`, `reviews`, and `contactForm` by vertical, not flat**: click-to-call and reviews weight highest for high-intent/trust-sensitive verticals (trades, auto, dental, law, spa); `contactForm` is primary for law and meaningful-secondary for trades/auto but low-value for cafe/barber/retail (and should distinguish a lead/intake/quote form from a newsletter signup).
- **Encode the two-layer trust model with Alberta/Calgary specifics**: a governing-credential slot for regulated verticals (AMVIC#, Law Society, ADA&C/CDSA registration, WCB+insurance, Calgary massage/RMT licence) plus a conversion-proof slot (reviews, direct-billing, warranty, consented before/after); cap badge count at 3–5 relevant credentials; flag prohibited copy (law "specialist"/superlatives/advertised verdicts; dental specialty/"best" claims).
- **Treat schema as display/AI-visibility, not a ranking or rich-result win**: audit for the correct most-specific subtype + name/address minimum and the small conditional-property map; don't credit FAQ schema as a SERP feature or self-authored `aggregateRating` as earning stars. Keep `localBusinessJsonLd` as a modest SEO-sub-score item rather than over-crediting it as on-page conversion.
